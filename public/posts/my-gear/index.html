My gear

The hardware and software I personally use

My students often consult me before making any guitar related purchases. I’ve never considered myself to be an expert on guitar gear, so tend to only suggest things I’m familiar with. In this post, I’ll talk briefly about all of my equipment, weighing up the pros and cons to each.

Hardware

As of right now I own:

  • 7 guitars
  • 3 pedals
  • 1 amplifier

Many guitarists have a much more extensive collection. I have a fair few students who own more pedals or guitars than me. Noteworthy guitars are:

Fender American Special Telecaster

I bought this guitar on Reverb in 2023. It was made in the US in 2012, and is probably the guitar that I play the most. It’s worth about £1000, and feels every bit as premium as you would hope. It is definitely the most versatile guitar I own, capable of anything from jazz to metal.

Fender Stratocaster ST-54 CIJ

I traded for this guitar in 2024. CIJ means ‘Crafted in Japan’, which it was in 1998. It’s a 1954 reissue with a V neck profile, making it quite clubby. I had never played a guitar like it, and knew quickly that I had to have it. I had a guitar of similar value sitting around collecting dust, so offered to trade. Accurately assessing its value is tricky because they don’t sell very often, but I’d estimate it to be worth from £750 to £1000.

Epiphone Sheraton-II PRO

I got this guitar in 2023. It was made in Korea in 2005. It plays and sounds great, and can be picked up around the £600 mark.

Ibanez RGAIX-7FM

I bought this 7 string Ibanez in 2017 with my first ever paycheque. I had started my first job the day after I turned 18 and knew exactly what I wanted to buy: A Chapman Ghost Fret. So why did I end up with an Ibanez? I went to Anderton’s in Guildford to try out some guitars, and decided that the Ghost Fret just wasn’t for me. It didn’t feel quite right, especially not for £1000. After looking around and trying out some other 7 string guitars, I settled on the Ibanez. I bought it brand new for £800, and you can grab one used today for around £650 - if you can find one. This model was discontinue in 2019, so can’t be bought new.

Gretsch G5260 Electromatic Jet Baritone

I always wanted to get a baritone guitar, but struggled to find any for a reasonable price. I wasn’t committed enough to spend a lot, so seeing one of these on reverb for £350 piqued my interest. I got it in 2023, and put on the thickest strings I possibly could. I keep it tuned to F standard (11 semitones down) and use it almost exclusively to cover songs that would otherwise require 8 strings. It doubles as a bass VI in a pinch.
